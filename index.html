About the Programme

Work Force Unit — National Programme

A private-led national infrastructure delivery mechanism that recruits, trains and deploys 5,000 Kenyan youth to execute priority infrastructure projects across all 47 counties.

🎯 Vision & Mission

Vision: A Kenya where no educated young person is unemployed for lack of a practical pathway, and no infrastructure project is delayed for lack of skilled labour.

Mission: Recruit, train and deploy a structured national workforce that bridges youth unemployment and infrastructure delivery — creating lasting socioeconomic value for participants, communities and the nation.

🏛️ How We Differ from NYS

WFU is NOT NYS. Key differences:
  • ✅ Private-led, independently governed — insulated from political risk
  • ✅ Output-contracted: km of road, metres of drain, poles installed
  • ✅ Self-financing from Month 8 via project revenue
  • ✅ TVET-certified, multi-skill training aligned to live projects
  • ✅ Quarterly independent audits — full public transparency

📬 How to Propose WFU to Government

The following is a structured approach to presenting the WFU proposal to relevant ministries or to the President's office if you have direct access.
🏛️ Step 1: Ministry of Labour & Social Protection
Present the proposal as a youth employment initiative aligned with Kenya's Bottom-Up Economic Transformation Agenda (BETA). Request a formal MoU for WFU to operate as an accredited workforce intermediary. Attach the full proposal and highlight the NYS differentiation.
🏗️ Step 2: Ministry of Roads & Infrastructure
Position WFU as a pre-qualified labor supply partner for national road projects. Request inclusion in the ministry's upcoming procurement framework as an organized labor contractor. Reference Ethiopia UIIDP and Rwanda HIMO precedents to demonstrate bankability.
⚡ Step 3: Kenya Power (KPLC) / REREC
Present WFU's electrical team capacity as a last-mile connectivity solution. Propose a sub-contractor arrangement for pole erection, LV line stringing and meter installation. Reference the 212-day connection backlog as the problem WFU solves.
🤝 Step 4: County Governments (CEC for Infrastructure)
Approach the County Executive Committee Member for Infrastructure in 3–5 priority counties. Offer a pilot: WFU teams upgrade one market centre as a demonstration project at reduced cost vs. open tender. Success case becomes the national template.
🏛️ If You Have Direct Access to H.E. The President

Request a brief (15-minute) private presentation through the State House Policy Unit or the Chief of Staff. Frame the WFU as a legacy infrastructure delivery mechanism — not a jobs scheme. Lead with three numbers: 5,000 jobs, 100km road in 6 months, self-financing from Month 8.

Connect WFU directly to the President's Bottom-Up Economic Transformation Agenda and Hustler Fund narrative. Emphasize that WFU builds the workforce capacity the agenda needs to deliver on the ground. Offer to pilot in 3 counties of H.E.'s choice as a proof of concept before national rollout.

Bring with you: a 2-page executive brief (not the full proposal), a laminated one-pager with the 4 key numbers, and a signed letter of intent from at least one county governor.
